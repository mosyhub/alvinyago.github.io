Introduction to Rotoscoping

What is Rotoscoping?

Rotoscoping is an animation technique where animators trace over live-action footage frame by frame to create realistic movement. The technique was first used by animator Max Fleischer in 1915, and has since been used in countless films, music videos, and commercials.

How Rotoscoping Works

The process typically involves:

  1. Capturing live-action footage
  2. Projecting frames onto a drawing surface
  3. Tracing the projected images
  4. Adjusting the artwork for stylistic purposes
  5. Compiling the traced frames into animation

Modern rotoscoping often uses digital tools and software like Adobe After Effects, Photoshop, or specialized rotoscoping applications.

Famous Examples

Rotoscoping has been used in many famous works:

  • Disney's "Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs" (1937)
  • Ralph Bakshi's "The Lord of the Rings" (1978)
  • A-ha's "Take On Me" music video (1985)
  • Richard Linklater's "Waking Life" (2001) and "A Scanner Darkly" (2006)
